§ I · The Three Reveals

What the chain sees, and what it never does.

Three properties that make a credential worth sealing — each verifiable on-chain, without exposing the person behind it.

01
visibility_off

Nothing personal on-chain

The ledger records only a nullifier and a bound address — no name, no grade, no course. The proof is the receipt; the data never leaves the holder.

Reveal · On-chain
02
fingerprint

One credential, one registration

A nullifier ties a single proof to a single identity. Reusing it across Sybil attempts is rejected — one seal, one claim, no duplicates.

Reveal · Anti-Sybil
03
verified_user

Selective disclosure

Prove you satisfy a predicate — “track matches,” “grade in range,” “issued before date” — while every other attribute stays strictly private.

Reveal · By request
§ II · The Distillation

From private attributes to a sealed proof.

Credentials enter as leaves in a Merkle tree; the issuer publishes a single root. A holder distills one fact into a proof, sealed and recorded — and the original data is never transmitted.
